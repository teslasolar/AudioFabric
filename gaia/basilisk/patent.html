3.2 Independent Claims Outline

Claim 1 (Composition of Matter — Broadest)

A dissolving microneedle patch comprising: (a) a biocompatible dissolving matrix forming a plurality of microneedles; (b) alagebrium or a pharmaceutically acceptable salt thereof; (c) poly-L-lactic acid microspheres dispersed within at least a portion of said microneedles; and (d) at least three peptide active agents selected from the group consisting of copper tripeptide-1, dipeptide diaminobutyroyl benzylamide diacetate, palmitoyl tripeptide-1, and palmitoyl tetrapeptide-7.

Claim 2 (Composition — Syn-Ake Specific)

The dissolving microneedle patch of Claim 1, wherein the at least three peptide active agents include dipeptide diaminobutyroyl benzylamide diacetate.

Claim 3 (Composition — Full Formulation)

The dissolving microneedle patch of Claim 1, further comprising: retinaldehyde, niacinamide, ascorbyl glucoside, sodium hyaluronate of at least two distinct molecular weights, and ceramide NP.

Claim 4 (Composition — With 5-ALA)

The dissolving microneedle patch of Claim 1, further comprising 5-aminolevulinic acid or a salt thereof.

Claim 5 (Method of Manufacture)

A method of manufacturing a dissolving microneedle patch, comprising: (a) casting a first solution comprising retinaldehyde at a pH below 5.0 into microneedle mold cavities to form needle tips; (b) partially drying said first solution; (c) casting a second solution comprising niacinamide and copper tripeptide-1 at a pH above 5.5 over said partially dried needle tips to form needle bodies; (d) casting a backing layer solution comprising alagebrium and poly-L-lactic acid microspheres; and (e) drying and demolding the resulting layered microneedle array.

Claim 6 (Method of Treatment — Broad)

A method of improving the appearance of aged or photodamaged skin, comprising: applying to a skin surface a dissolving microneedle patch comprising alagebrium or a salt thereof, whereby the microneedles penetrate the stratum corneum and dissolve to release alagebrium into the dermis, thereby cleaving advanced glycation end-product crosslinks in dermal collagen.

Claim 7 (Method of Treatment — Combination)

The method of Claim 6, further comprising delivering 5-aminolevulinic acid via said microneedle patch and subsequently exposing the treated skin to light in the range of 400-450 nm to induce protoporphyrin IX-mediated collagen remodeling.

7.0 Trade Secret vs. Patent Analysis

Subject MatterRecommended ProtectionRationale
Core composition (ALT-711 + peptides + PLLA in MN)PATENTComposition is discoverable by reverse engineering (analytical chemistry of finished product). Patent provides offensive protection and licensing opportunity.
Layered casting process (pH separation)PATENT + TRADE SECRET (details)Patent broad method claims; keep specific parameters (temperatures, times, concentrations) as trade secrets in manufacturing SOPs.
PLLA microsphere preparationTRADE SECRETMicrosphere size distribution and surface treatment are difficult to reverse engineer. Process know-how provides competitive advantage.
Mold fabrication parametersTRADE SECRETMaster mold production parameters (laser settings, PDMS ratios, curing profiles) are not detectable in finished product.
ALT-711 synthesis / purificationTRADE SECRETIf synthesizing in-house. If purchasing from supplier, not applicable.
Stability formulation (excipient ratios for shelf life)TRADE SECRETSpecific ratios optimized through stability studies; difficult to reverse engineer exact formulation.
QC methods (dissolution testing, potency assay)TRADE SECRETAnalytical methods not detectable in product. Keep as internal SOPs.
