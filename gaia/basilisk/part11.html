This document defines the compliance framework for electronic records and electronic signatures per 21 CFR Part 11, as applicable to BASILISK manufacturing, quality, and regulatory systems.

1.0 Purpose

To establish the policies, procedures, and technical controls necessary to ensure that electronic records and electronic signatures used in the manufacture and quality management of the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch comply with 21 CFR Part 11 requirements. This document defines the framework for system validation, access controls, audit trails, electronic signatures, and data integrity across all GxP-regulated electronic systems.

2.2 Exclusions

The following are excluded from Part 11 scope: general business email (non-GxP), financial/accounting systems, building management systems (non-cleanroom), and personal productivity tools not used to create or manage GxP records.

4.0 Electronic Signatures

4.1 Meaning and Legal Binding

Electronic signatures applied within Part 11-compliant systems shall be the legally binding equivalent of handwritten signatures per 21 CFR 11.50. Each organization using electronic signatures shall submit a certification letter to the FDA prior to or at the time of first electronic signature use.

8.0 Data Integrity — ALCOA+ Principles

All GxP data generated in electronic systems shall comply with ALCOA+ principles as defined by FDA and WHO guidance:

A — Attributable
Every data entry must be traceable to the person who performed it. System-generated data must be traceable to the instrument/system. No anonymous or shared-account entries permitted.

L — Legible
All records must be readable and permanent throughout their retention period. Electronic records must be viewable in human-readable format. Printouts, if used as official records, must be legible.

C — Contemporaneous
Data must be recorded at the time the activity is performed. Systems shall enforce real-time data entry where feasible. Backdating of entries is prohibited; late entries must be documented with justification.

O — Original
The first capture of data is the original record. For electronic systems, the electronic record is the original (not a printout). Original data must be preserved and accessible. True copies must be verified as exact and complete.

A — Accurate
Data must be error-free and conform to the actual observation or measurement. Systems shall include checks (e.g., range checks, mandatory fields) to prevent transcription errors. Corrections must preserve the original entry.

+ Complete
All data, including repeat tests, OOS results, and failed runs, must be retained. Deletion of GxP data is prohibited. All relevant metadata must be preserved.

+ Consistent
Data elements must be internally consistent (e.g., timestamps in chronological order). Date/time stamps must reflect the actual sequence of events.

+ Enduring
Records must be retained and accessible for the required retention period. Media must be periodically verified for readability. Migration plans required for system decommissioning.

+ Available
Records must be accessible and retrievable throughout their retention period. Backup and archival systems must allow timely retrieval. Records must be available for inspection upon request.
