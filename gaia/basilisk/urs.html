1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 Purpose

This User Requirements Specification (URS) defines the user needs, performance criteria, and acceptance requirements for the BASILISK™ dissolving microneedle patch system. This document serves as the foundational design input per 21 CFR 820.30(c) and ISO 13485:2016 clause 7.3.3.

1.2 Scope

This URS covers both product variants:
 V1 BASILISK CROWN — 10-ingredient cosmetic microneedle device (Class II)
 V2 BASILISK RADIANT — 11-ingredient combination drug-device product (with 5-ALA)
Requirements applicable to specific versions are annotated accordingly. Unmarked requirements apply to both.

1.3 Applicable Standards

21 CFR 820.30 — Design Controls
ISO 13485:2016 — Medical Devices, Quality Management Systems
GAMP 5 — Good Automated Manufacturing Practice (URS framework)
IEC 62366-1:2015 — Usability Engineering
ISO 14971:2019 — Risk Management for Medical Devices

2. INTENDED USE AND INDICATIONS

2.1 Intended Use Statement

BASILISK™ is a dissolving microneedle patch intended for transdermal delivery of cosmetic and/or therapeutic agents to the dermis for the purpose of promoting dermal collagen repair, hydration, and skin rejuvenation.

2.2 Indications for Use

V1 (Device): For temporary improvement in the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles through dermal hydration and support of the skin's natural collagen renewal processes.
V2 (Drug-Device): For the treatment of moderate to severe photoaging, including fine lines, wrinkles, and loss of skin elasticity, through targeted dermal delivery of collagen-stimulating agents with photodynamic activation.

2.3 Intended Users

V1: Self-application by adult consumers (18+) in a home-use setting. No clinical supervision required.
V2: Initial use under healthcare provider supervision; potential transition to home use following training.

2.4 Target Population

Adults aged 30-70 with visible signs of skin photoaging (Glogau Scale II-IV).
Exclusions: Patients with known hypersensitivity to hyaluronic acid, PLLA, or any listed component; active skin infections at the application site; pregnancy or lactation; patients on anticoagulant therapy (relative); history of keloid scarring.

2.5 Anatomical Target

Periorbital region, nasolabial folds, forehead, décolletage. Patch geometry optimized for facial contours.
