1. SCOPE & PURPOSE

Product: BASILISK™ Dissolving Microneedle Collagen Repair System (V1 CROWN / V2 RADIANT)
Scope: This risk management file covers the entire product lifecycle from design through post-market surveillance, per ISO 14971:2019 Clause 4.1. Applies to both V1 (10-ingredient cosmetic device) and V2 (11-ingredient combination drug-device with 5-ALA).
Applicable standards: ISO 14971:2019, ISO/TR 24971:2020 (guidance), IEC 62366-1:2015+A1:2020 (usability), 21 CFR 820.30(g)
Risk management activities: hazard identification, risk estimation, risk evaluation, risk control, residual risk evaluation, risk-benefit analysis, production and post-production review

2.2 Risk Acceptability Criteria

Policy: No individual risk shall have a Risk Priority Number (RPN) > 100 after controls. Risks with severity = 5 (death/permanent injury) require independent review regardless of probability.
Risk LevelRPN RangeAction Required
UNACCEPTABLERPN ≥ 100Risk reduction mandatory. Design change or additional controls required before release.
ALARA50 ≤ RPN < 100As Low As Reasonably Achievable. Reduce if practicable; document rationale if retained.
ACCEPTABLE20 ≤ RPN < 50Acceptable with monitoring. Include in post-market surveillance plan.
BROADLY ACCEPTABLERPN < 20No further action required. Document in risk file.

2.3 Risk Estimation Parameters

RPN = Severity (S) × Probability (P) × Detectability (D)
Each factor scored 1–5. Maximum RPN = 125.

6. RISK CONTROL MEASURES

Control hierarchy per ISO 14971:2019 Clause 7: (1) Inherently safe design → (2) Protective measures → (3) Information for safety

6.1 Design Controls (Inherently Safe Design)

DC-01 — HA:PLLA matrix ratio optimized to 70:30 w/w for complete dissolution in ≤15 min across Fitzpatrick skin types I–VI
DC-02 — Needle geometry: 500μm height, 200μm base, 10μm tip radius — engineered to penetrate stratum corneum without reaching pain-sensing dermis depth (>700μm)
DC-03 — Retinol microencapsulation in PLLA microspheres (5–15μm) provides UV shielding + controlled release over 6 months
DC-04 — GHK-Cu stabilization via lyophilized dry state + histidine co-formulation to prevent Cu dissociation
DC-05 — ALT-711 anhydrous stability: lyophilized at <3% moisture eliminates hydrolysis pathway; t½ extends from 14 days (aqueous) to >24 months (dry)
DC-06 — Physical separation of pH-incompatible ingredients: retinol in PLLA core, niacinamide in HA shell — no contact until in vivo dissolution
DC-07 — 5-ALA dose limitation (V2): 0.2% concentration vs 20% in photodynamic therapy — 100× safety margin against phototoxic threshold
DC-08 — EGF concentration limited to 1 ppm (equivalent to physiologic wound-healing levels) to minimize tumorigenic risk

6.2 Process Controls (Protective Measures)

PC-01 — Terminal sterilization: e-beam 25 kGy per ISO 11137 (validated dose) OR aseptic fill in ISO 5 cleanroom
PC-02 — 100% needle integrity inspection: automated vision system checks each mold for broken/missing needles prior to packaging
PC-03 — Moisture control: lyophilization to <3% w/w residual moisture; Karl Fischer verification per lot
PC-04 — Nitrogen flush + oxygen scavenger in sealed foil pouch; headspace O₂ <0.5% verified per lot
PC-05 — 100% seal integrity test: vacuum leak detection per ASTM F2095 + visual inspection
PC-06 — HPLC release testing: each ingredient ≥90% and ≤110% of label claim; degradation products ≤2% individual, ≤5% total
PC-07 — Bioburden pre-sterilization: <10 CFU/device; USP <71> sterility test per lot
PC-08 — Endotoxin testing: LAL per USP <85>; limit ≤20 EU/device

6.3 Labeling Controls (Information for Safety)

LC-01 — Contraindications (bold, front panel): “Do not use if pregnant, nursing, immunocompromised, or under 18 years of age”
LC-02 — Application instructions with pictograms: clean dry skin → press firmly 10 sec → leave 15 min → remove → discard
LC-03 — Patch test instruction: “Apply test patch to inner forearm 24 hours before first facial use”
LC-04 — Sun avoidance: “Avoid direct sun exposure for 24 hours after application” (48 hours for V2 with 5-ALA)
LC-05 — Eye contact warning: “Do not apply within 2 cm of eye. If contact with eye occurs, rinse with water for 15 minutes”
LC-06 — Single use only: symbol per ISO 7000-1051 + text “SINGLE USE — DO NOT REUSE”
LC-07 — Storage conditions: “Store at 15–25°C. Protect from light. Do not freeze”
LC-08 — Drug interaction warning: “Do not use with topical retinoids, AHAs, BHAs, or chemical peels within 72 hours”
LC-09 — Full ingredient list per INCI nomenclature + MoCRA compliance (21 CFR Part 740 equivalent)

8. RISK-BENEFIT ANALYSIS

8.1 Clinical Benefits

B-01 — AGE crosslink reversal: ALT-711 is the only known compound that cleaves established glycation crosslinks in collagen. No topical or injectable equivalent exists. Addresses root cause of age-related skin stiffening, not just symptoms.
B-02 — Multi-mechanism collagen repair: 11 ingredients target 7 distinct pathways (AGE breaking, fibroblast stimulation, neuromodulation, hydration, photoactivation, matrix remodeling, growth factor signaling). Superior to single-mechanism treatments.
B-03 — Non-surgical alternative: replaces injectable fillers, Botox, and ablative procedures. No anesthesia, no downtime, no practitioner required (V1).
B-04 — Sustained release: PLLA microsphere platform delivers actives over 6–12 months from a single 15-minute application. Current microneedling requires monthly professional sessions.
B-05 — Dissolving delivery: eliminates sharps waste and needle-stick injury risk inherent to metal/silicon microneedle systems.

8.2 Risk-Benefit Conclusion

V1 BASILISK CROWN: All residual risks are broadly acceptable (max RPN = 12). Clinical benefits of non-invasive, multi-pathway collagen repair substantially outweigh risks of transient local reactions. The benefit-risk profile is FAVORABLE. ACCEPTABLE
V2 BASILISK RADIANT: Addition of 5-ALA introduces phototoxicity risk (FM-12, residual RPN = 8) mitigated by 100× dose reduction from PDT levels and mandatory sun avoidance labeling. Incremental benefit of photoactivated collagen remodeling justifies incremental risk under Rx-only dispensing. The benefit-risk profile is FAVORABLE with conditions. ACCEPTABLE (Rx-only)

9. PRODUCTION & POST-PRODUCTION MONITORING

9.1 Post-Market Surveillance Plan

Complaint handling: All user complaints triaged within 24 hours; adverse events reported per 21 CFR 803 (MDR) within 30 days (serious: 5 days)
Periodic review: Risk management file reviewed annually or upon: (a) new complaint trend, (b) CAPA closure, (c) design change, (d) new clinical evidence
Signal detection: Statistical monitoring of complaint rates per lot; CUSUM analysis for trend detection
Clinical follow-up: Post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) per EU MDR Article 61; clinical evaluation report (CER) updated biennially

9.2 Risk File Maintenance

Trigger events for risk file update:
● New adverse event type not in hazard identification
● Complaint rate exceeds 0.1% of units distributed
● Severity upgrade based on post-market evidence
● Design or manufacturing process change (via change control)
● New regulatory guidance or harmonized standard revision
● Supplier change for any active ingredient
● New published literature on ingredient safety
