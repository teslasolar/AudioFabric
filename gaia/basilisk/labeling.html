This document specifies labeling content, format, and regulatory requirements for all BASILISK product variants and target markets.

1.0 Purpose

To define the labeling requirements for the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch in compliance with applicable FDA regulations (21 CFR 801, 21 CFR 701), MoCRA cosmetic labeling provisions, EU MDR/Cosmetics Regulation, and international labeling standards. This specification covers all label elements from outer carton to individual pouch to instructions for use (IFU).

7.0 Instructions for Use (IFU)

7.1 Application Procedure

StepInstructionDurationCritical Note
1Cleanse treatment area with mild cleanser. Pat dry completely.2 minSkin must be completely dry — moisture affects needle dissolution rate
2Open foil pouch along tear notch. Remove patch from protective liner.30 secHandle by edges only. Do not touch needle surface.
3Place patch on treatment area with needle side against skin.10 secPosition before pressing — do not slide or reposition after contact
4Press firmly and evenly across entire patch surface with fingertips.30 secApply approximately 1-2 N/cm² pressure. You may feel brief tingling.
5Maintain patch in place. Do not disturb.15 minNeedles dissolve during this period. Patch may be secured with medical tape if needed.
6Gently peel backing from one corner.15 secNeedles should be fully dissolved. If white residue remains, pat gently — do not rub.
7Dispose of used backing in household waste. Wash hands.—Do not flush. Do not reuse.

8.0 Contraindications, Warnings & Precautions

8.1 Contraindications

ContraindicationRationaleApplies To
Pregnancy or planned pregnancyRetinaldehyde is a known teratogen (Vitamin A derivative). Systemic absorption via microneedle delivery is possible.V1, V2
BreastfeedingInsufficient data on excretion of microneedle-delivered actives in breast milk.V1, V2
Known hypersensitivity to any ingredientAllergic contact dermatitis risk. Copper allergy precludes GHK-Cu exposure.V1, V2
Active skin infection at treatment siteMicroneedle penetration may spread infection or introduce bacteria to deeper skin layers.V1, V2
Immunocompromised patientsImpaired wound healing; increased infection risk from micropunctures.V1, V2
Porphyria or photosensitivity disorders5-ALA induces protoporphyrin IX; contraindicated in porphyria patients.V2 only
Concurrent use of photosensitizing drugsTetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, thiazides, phenothiazines — additive photosensitivity with 5-ALA.V2 only

8.2 Warnings

WarningDetail
For external use onlyDo not ingest. Do not apply to mucous membranes, eyes, or lips.
Single use onlyEach patch is for one-time use. Do not attempt to reapply a used patch. Microneedles dissolve upon first application.
Retinoid sensitivityContains retinaldehyde. May cause redness, peeling, or sensitivity. Discontinue use if severe irritation occurs. Use sunscreen (SPF 30+) during daytime when using this product.
Sun exposure (V2)Avoid direct sun exposure for 48 hours after application. 5-ALA causes transient photosensitivity.
Skin reactionsMild erythema, tingling, or warmth at application site is expected and typically resolves within 2-4 hours. Seek medical attention if swelling, blistering, or persistent pain occurs.
Keep out of reach of childrenMicroneedles pose puncture risk. This is not a toy.

8.3 ALT-711 (Alagebrium) Disclosure

IMPORTANT NOTICE — ALT-711 / ALAGEBRIUM CHLORIDE

This product contains alagebrium chloride (ALT-711), an advanced glycation end-product (AGE) crosslink breaker. ALT-711 was originally developed by Alteon Inc. for cardiovascular indications and completed Phase II clinical trials (systemic oral administration) before development was discontinued for commercial reasons.

The mechanism of ALT-711 involves cleavage of alpha-diketone crosslinks between proteins. ALT-711 does NOT cleave glucosepane crosslinks, which represent the predominant AGE crosslink in aged human skin collagen. The contribution of alpha-diketone (non-glucosepane) crosslinks to skin mechanical properties and aging appearance is an area of active research.

Topical dermal delivery of ALT-711 via microneedle has not been previously studied. Safety and efficacy data for this route of administration are based on the manufacturer's internal studies. This product has not been evaluated by the FDA for safety or efficacy.

9.2 V2 Drug Claims

V2 drug claims will be defined in the FDA-approved prescribing information and are limited to the exact indications and language approved in the NDA. No off-label promotion permitted. Claims will be developed during Phase II based on clinical endpoints.
