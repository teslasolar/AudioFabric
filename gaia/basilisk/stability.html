This document defines the stability study design for BASILISK dissolving microneedle patches per ICH, ASTM, and FDA guidance.

1.0 Purpose

To establish the protocol for long-term, accelerated, and photostability testing of the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch (drug-device combination product) in accordance with ICH Q1A(R2), ICH Q1B, and ASTM F1980. This protocol applies to all formulation variants and ensures shelf-life substantiation for regulatory submission.

2.0 Scope

This protocol covers stability evaluation of the finished BASILISK patch (drug substance within dissolving microneedle array on backing substrate) in its primary packaging configuration. Both storage variants are included:

VariantStorage ConditionPrimary PackagingActive
V1 (Room Temp)15-25 CFoil pouch with desiccant5-ALA HCl
V2 (Cold Chain)2-8 CFoil pouch, secondary carton5-ALA HCl

4.2 ASTM F1980 — Accelerated Aging (Device Component)

The backing substrate, adhesive border, and primary packaging (foil pouch) shall undergo accelerated aging per ASTM F1980 to validate package integrity and device functionality over the claimed shelf life.

ParameterValue
Accelerated aging temperature (TAA)55 C
Ambient temperature (TRT)25 C
Q10 factor2.0 (conservative)
Target shelf life18 months
Accelerated aging duration≥ 2.12 months (per Q10 calculation)
Real-time aging (parallel)18 months

Accelerated aging does not replace real-time aging. Both run concurrently; real-time data is required for final shelf-life claims.

4.3 ICH Q1B — Photostability

5-ALA is photosensitive. Photostability testing is critical and shall be performed under the confirmatory study conditions defined in ICH Q1B Option 2.

ExposureCondition
Visible light≥ 1.2 million lux hours
Near-UV≥ 200 watt hours/m2
ControlsDark control (wrapped in foil), light-exposed in primary pack, light-exposed without primary pack

8.0 Storage Condition Rationale

VariantLabeled StorageRationale
V1 (Room Temp)15-25 C, protect from light and moistureFoil pouch with desiccant provides moisture and light barrier. Stability data at 25 C/60% RH must support 18-month claim. Accelerated data at 40 C/75% RH provides early prediction of shelf life.
V2 (Cold Chain)2-8 C, protect from lightCold storage extends stability margin for photosensitive active. Refrigerated long-term study at 5 C supports label claim. Accelerated study at 25 C/60% RH evaluates short-term excursion tolerance.

9.0 Out-of-Specification (OOS) Procedures

Any result falling outside the acceptance criteria listed in Section 7.0 shall be investigated per the site OOS SOP (QA-SOP-012). Investigation shall include:

StepActionResponsible
9.1Analyst self-check: verify calculations, instrument calibration, sample preparationAnalyst
9.2Supervisor review of raw data and laboratory conditionsLab Supervisor
9.3If assignable cause found, retest per OOS SOPQC
9.4If no assignable cause, initiate Phase II manufacturing investigationQA / Manufacturing
9.5Evaluate impact on shelf-life claim; update stability reportQA / RA
