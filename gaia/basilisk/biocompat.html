1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

Purpose: Define the biocompatibility evaluation strategy for the BASILISK™ Dissolving Microneedle Collagen Repair System per ISO 10993-1:2018, including material characterization, required biological tests, acceptance criteria, and testing strategy.
Product: BASILISK™ V1 CROWN (10 ingredients) and V2 RADIANT (11 ingredients, includes 5-ALA)
Device description: Dissolving microneedle array (7×7, 49 needles) on adhesive backing. Needles composed of hyaluronic acid / poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA) composite matrix loaded with 9 active ingredients + 2 peptide neuromodulators. Needles dissolve in situ within 15 minutes, depositing actives into dermis.
Applicable standards: ISO 10993-1:2018, ISO 10993-3, -5, -6, -10, -11, -12, -17, -18, -23; FDA Guidance “Use of ISO 10993-1” (2020); EU MDR 2017/745 Annex I §10.4

3.3 Chemical Characterization Requirements (ISO 10993-18:2020)

CC-01 — Extractables study: exhaustive extraction (polar + nonpolar solvents) per ISO 10993-12:2021 on finished device. GC-MS, LC-MS/MS, ICP-MS for metals.
CC-02 — Leachables study: simulated use extraction (phosphate-buffered saline, pH 7.4, 37°C, 24 hr) mimicking in vivo dissolution conditions.
CC-03 — Degradation product identification: forced degradation (acid, base, oxidative, photolytic, thermal) with LC-MS/MS characterization of all degradants ≥0.1%.
CC-04 — Elemental impurities: per ICH Q3D(R2) / ISO 10993-17:2023; limits per parenteral route for intradermal delivery.
CC-05 — Residual solvent analysis: per ICH Q3C(R8) for any solvents used in PLLA microsphere fabrication (dichloromethane if emulsion method).
CC-06 — Particle characterization: PLLA microsphere size distribution (laser diffraction), morphology (SEM), crystallinity (DSC/XRD).

4. REQUIRED BIOLOGICAL TESTS (ISO 10993-1:2018 TABLE A.1)

Device category: Breached/compromised surface + implant component (PLLA). Duration: Limited (matrix) + Permanent (PLLA microspheres).
Testing basis: Permanent implant endpoints apply because PLLA microspheres reside in tissue >30 days.

4.1 — ISO 10993-5: CYTOTOXICITY

Purpose: Evaluate potential of device materials and extractables to cause cell death or growth inhibition
Method: MEM (Minimum Essential Medium) elution assay per ISO 10993-5:2009
Cell line: L929 mouse fibroblasts (ATCC CCL-1)
Extraction conditions: Complete device extracted in MEM + 10% FBS, 37°C, 24 hr (per ISO 10993-12:2021 surface area / volume ratio)
Endpoints: Cell viability (quantitative: XTT or MTT metabolic assay), morphological assessment (qualitative: grade 0–4)
Additional: Direct contact assay for PLLA microsphere component (cells seeded on microspheres, 24 hr + 72 hr)
ACCEPTANCE: Cell viability ≥70% of negative control (quantitative). Morphological grade ≤2 (mild reactivity) for qualitative. Positive control (latex) must show grade ≥3.

4.2 — ISO 10993-10 / ISO 10993-23: IRRITATION & SKIN SENSITIZATION

Purpose: Evaluate potential to cause skin irritation and delayed-type hypersensitivity (Type IV)
4.2.1 Irritation
Method: Reconstructed Human Epidermis (RhE) model per ISO 10993-23:2021 (replaces Draize rabbit test)
Model: EpiDerm™ (MatTek) or EpiSkin™ (L'Oréal) — validated OECD TG 439
Extraction: Device extract (saline + sesame oil) applied to RhE tissue for 60 min exposure
Endpoints: Tissue viability (MTT assay), IL-1α release (ELISA)
ACCEPTANCE: Mean tissue viability ≥50% of negative control (non-irritant classification per OECD TG 439). IL-1α <100 pg/mL.
4.2.2 Skin Sensitization
Method: Combination of three validated non-animal methods (Defined Approach per OECD GL 497):
(a) DPRA (Direct Peptide Reactivity Assay) — OECD TG 442C: cysteine and lysine peptide depletion
(b) KeratinoSens™ — OECD TG 442D: ARE-Nrf2 luciferase reporter in HaCaT keratinocytes
(c) h-CLAT (Human Cell Line Activation Test) — OECD TG 442E: CD86/CD54 expression on THP-1 cells
Supplemental: RIPT (Repeated Insult Patch Test) on ≥200 human subjects for finished device (clinical confirmation)
ACCEPTANCE: DPRA: cysteine depletion <13.89% AND lysine depletion <23.03% (non-sensitizer). KeratinoSens: EC1.5 >1000 μM (negative). h-CLAT: CD86 <150% and CD54 <200% of control (negative). RIPT: zero sensitization reactions in ≥200 subjects.

4.3 — ISO 10993-3: GENOTOXICITY

Purpose: Evaluate potential of device materials and extractables to cause gene mutations, chromosomal damage, or DNA damage
Rationale: Required for all implant devices with >24 hr contact. PLLA microspheres qualify as permanent implant.
4.3.1 Gene Mutation (Ames Test)
Method: Bacterial reverse mutation assay per OECD TG 471
Strains: S. typhimurium TA98, TA100, TA1535, TA1537 + E. coli WP2 uvrA
Conditions: ± S9 metabolic activation; device extract (polar + nonpolar); 5 concentrations + vehicle + positive controls
ACCEPTANCE: <2× increase in revertant colonies vs vehicle control at all concentrations, with no dose-response. Positive controls must show ≥3× increase.
4.3.2 Chromosomal Damage (In Vitro Micronucleus)
Method: In vitro mammalian cell micronucleus test per OECD TG 487
Cell line: CHO-K1 or TK6 human lymphoblastoid cells
Conditions: ± S9; short-term (3 hr + 24 hr recovery) and continuous (24 hr) exposure; ≥2000 cells scored per concentration
ACCEPTANCE: No statistically significant increase (p >0.05, Fisher's exact test) in micronucleated cells vs vehicle control. Positive control (mitomycin C/colchicine) must show significant increase.

4.4 — ISO 10993-6: IMPLANTATION (LOCAL TISSUE EFFECTS)

Purpose: Evaluate local tissue response to PLLA microspheres and dissolved needle matrix deposited in dermis
Rationale: CRITICAL — Dissolving microneedles deposit material into living tissue. PLLA microspheres remain for 6–12 months. This is the most important biocompatibility endpoint for BASILISK.
Method: Subcutaneous / intradermal implantation per ISO 10993-6:2016
Animal model: New Zealand White rabbit (intradermal) + Sprague-Dawley rat (subcutaneous for PLLA microspheres)
Test articles:
(a) Complete BASILISK patch applied to shaved dorsal skin (rabbit intradermal model)
(b) PLLA microspheres injected subcutaneously (rat model, 12-month endpoint)
(c) HA matrix alone (negative control comparison)
(d) USP-grade HDPE strip (negative control per ISO 10993-6)
Timepoints: 1 week, 4 weeks, 12 weeks, 26 weeks, 52 weeks (PLLA only)
Evaluation: Gross pathology, histopathology (H&E, Masson's trichrome for collagen, CD68 for macrophages), semi-quantitative scoring per ISO 10993-6 Annex E
ACCEPTANCE: Test article tissue response score ≤ negative control score + 2.9 at each timepoint (non-irritant per ISO 10993-6 Annex E scoring). No abscess, necrosis, or excessive fibrosis. Foreign body giant cell reaction, if present, must be minimal (grade ≤2) and decreasing over time. PLLA microspheres must show progressive resorption without chronic inflammation at 52 weeks.

4.5 — ISO 10993-11: SYSTEMIC TOXICITY

Purpose: Evaluate potential for systemic toxic effects from absorbed device materials
Rationale: Required for implant devices. ALT-711 and peptide components have limited intradermal absorption data.
4.5.1 Acute Systemic Toxicity
Method: Per ISO 10993-11:2017 Annex B; USP <87> / <88> Class VI
Animal model: Swiss albino mice (5/sex/group); IV and IP routes
Dose: Maximum feasible dose of device extract (50 mL/kg polar; 10 mL/kg nonpolar)
Observation: 72 hours; clinical signs, body weight, gross necropsy
ACCEPTANCE: No mortality. No significant body weight loss (>10%). No significant clinical signs (convulsions, prostration, respiratory distress). Gross necropsy: no organ abnormalities.
4.5.2 Subchronic Systemic Toxicity (28-day)
Method: Per ISO 10993-11:2017 Annex D; OECD TG 407
Animal model: Sprague-Dawley rats (10/sex/group)
Dose groups: Low (1× clinical equivalent), Mid (5×), High (25×), Vehicle control
Route: Intradermal injection of device extract (to mimic dissolved microneedle delivery)
Duration: 28 days repeated dose + 14 days recovery
Endpoints: Clinical observations, body weight, food consumption, hematology (CBC + differential), clinical chemistry (ALT, AST, BUN, creatinine, albumin, glucose), urinalysis, gross necropsy, organ weights, histopathology (liver, kidney, spleen, injection site, lymph nodes)
ACCEPTANCE: NOAEL (No Observed Adverse Effect Level) ≥25× clinical dose. No target organ toxicity. No treatment-related mortality. Hematology and clinical chemistry within ±20% of control group means with no dose-response trend.
4.5.3 Subchronic Systemic Toxicity (90-day) — PLLA Component
Method: Per ISO 10993-11:2017; OECD TG 408
Rationale: Required for permanent implant (>30 days contact). PLLA microspheres degrade over 6–12 months; 90-day study captures chronic lactic acid release phase.
Design: Same as 28-day study with 90-day exposure + 28-day recovery; subcutaneous PLLA microsphere implantation in rats
ACCEPTANCE: Same criteria as 28-day study. Additionally: no evidence of systemic lactic acidosis (serum lactate within normal range); no hepatotoxicity or nephrotoxicity attributable to PLLA degradation products.

4.7 — ISO 10993-23: IRRITATION TESTING (UPDATED METHODS)

Purpose: Updated irritation testing methodology replacing the in vivo Draize test with validated in vitro RhE models
Reference: ISO 10993-23:2021 — Tests for irritation (supersedes relevant sections of ISO 10993-10 for irritation)
Method: Reconstructed Human Epidermis (RhE) model per OECD TG 439
Test protocol:
1. Prepare polar and nonpolar extracts per ISO 10993-12:2021
2. Apply 50 μL extract to RhE tissue surface (EpiDerm™ EPI-200)
3. Incubate 60 min at 37°C, 5% CO₂, 95% RH
4. Rinse, transfer to fresh medium, incubate 42 hr (recovery)
5. MTT viability assay; calculate mean % viability vs negative control
6. Classify per threshold: ≥50% viability = non-irritant; <50% = irritant
Supplemental ocular irritation: EpiOcular™ Eye Irritation Test (OECD TG 492) for accidental eye contact risk characterization
ACCEPTANCE: Mean tissue viability ≥50% (non-irritant classification). For ocular: viability ≥60% (no eye irritation category per GHS). Both positive and negative controls must meet defined acceptance ranges.

5. TESTING STRATEGY

5.1 Approach: Leverage Existing Data Where Possible

Principle: Per ISO 10993-1:2018 Clause 4.3 and FDA Guidance (2020), existing data may reduce or eliminate the need for new testing when: (a) the material has established biocompatibility in equivalent contact conditions, (b) the concentration is within previously tested ranges, and (c) the manufacturing process does not introduce new risks.
IngredientData SufficiencyStrategy
Hyaluronic AcidSUFFICIENTCite existing ISO 10993 data from dermal filler device master files. No new testing for HA alone.
PLLASUFFICIENTCite Sculptra (Galderma) predicate data + published literature. New implantation study required for microsphere form factor only.
RetinolSUFFICIENTCIR safety assessment + GRAS status. Intradermal exposure covered by published transdermal PK studies. Literature review.
NiacinamideSUFFICIENTCIR + GRAS. Extensive oral and topical safety data. Literature review sufficient.
GHK-CuPARTIALTopical safety data available. New: cytotoxicity + sensitization at intradermal concentration. Cu ion release quantification required.
ALT-711INSUFFICIENTPhase II oral trial data (Alteon Inc.) provides systemic tox baseline. New: full ISO 10993 panel required for intradermal route. Priority testing.
MatrixylPARTIALTopical safety data. New: cytotoxicity + genotoxicity at intradermal concentration.
ArgirelinePARTIALCIR reviewed topically. New: intradermal systemic toxicity assessment.
Syn-AkeINSUFFICIENTTopical cosmetic only. Venom-mimetic mechanism requires full safety panel for intradermal. New: complete ISO 10993 battery.
EGFPARTIALWound healing product data at therapeutic doses. New: genotoxicity and carcinogenicity risk assessment at 1 ppm intradermal. Literature-based biological risk assessment per ISO 10993-1 Clause 4.4.
5-ALA (V2)SUFFICIENTFDA-approved drug (Levulan). Comprehensive safety data at 100× higher dose. Literature review + concentration-adjusted risk assessment.

5.2 Novel Combination Testing

⚠ Even where individual ingredients have established safety, the combination in a dissolving microneedle matrix is novel. ISO 10993-1:2018 Clause 4.6 requires testing of the finished device, not just individual components. All Part 5, 10, 23, and 11 (acute) tests must be performed on complete device extracts.
Priority novel combinations requiring dedicated testing:
NC-01 — ALT-711 + GHK-Cu interaction: ALT-711 thiazolium ring may chelate copper from GHK-Cu. Test: cytotoxicity of combined extract vs individual extracts.
NC-02 — Retinol + HA matrix stability: HA hydration may accelerate retinol oxidation. Test: 6-month stability with degradation product toxicological assessment.
NC-03 — Syn-Ake intradermal absorption: Topical safety does not predict intradermal safety for a neuromuscular agent. Test: in vitro transdermal PK (Franz cell) + in vivo PK in rat model.
NC-04 — PLLA microsphere + peptide adsorption: Peptides may adsorb to PLLA surface, altering release kinetics and creating concentrated local exposure. Test: dissolution profile + local tissue response at peak concentration.

6.2 Highest-Risk Ingredients

ALT-711 (Alagebrium): No prior intradermal or topical use. Oral Phase II data (Alteon, 2003–2007) showed hepatotoxicity signals at 210 mg/day oral. Estimated dermal dose: 0.4 mg per patch application. Safety margin: >500× vs oral LOAEL. However, intradermal bioavailability is unknown and may differ significantly from oral. FULL TESTING REQUIRED
Syn-Ake: Waglerin-1 mimetic peptide. Neuromuscular mechanism at nicotinic acetylcholine receptor. No intradermal safety data published. Topical penetration is negligible; microneedle delivery bypasses barrier entirely. Systemic absorption and neuromuscular effects at intradermal dose must be characterized. FULL TESTING REQUIRED
EGF: Theoretical tumorigenic concern at supraphysiologic doses. BASILISK concentration (1 ppm / 0.0001%) is at physiologic wound-healing levels. Literature-based risk assessment per ISO 10993-1 Clause 4.4 may suffice if supporting data is robust. RISK ASSESSMENT + LITERATURE REVIEW

9. TESTING TIMELINE & SEQUENCING

9.1 Phase 1: Screening (Months 1–3)

● Chemical characterization (ISO 10993-18): extractables/leachables — 8 weeks
● Cytotoxicity (ISO 10993-5): MEM elution + direct contact — 4 weeks
● Genotoxicity (ISO 10993-3): Ames + micronucleus — 8 weeks
● Irritation (ISO 10993-23): RhE model — 4 weeks

9.2 Phase 2: Core Battery (Months 3–6)

● Sensitization (ISO 10993-10): DPRA + KeratinoSens + h-CLAT — 6 weeks
● Acute systemic toxicity (ISO 10993-11): mouse IV/IP — 6 weeks
● 28-day subchronic toxicity (ISO 10993-11): rat — 12 weeks (including pathology)

9.3 Phase 3: Long-Term Studies (Months 6–18)

● Implantation (ISO 10993-6): rabbit + rat, up to 52-week endpoint — 14 months
● 90-day subchronic toxicity (ISO 10993-11): rat with PLLA — 20 weeks
● RIPT clinical sensitization: 200+ subjects — 12 weeks

9.4 Phase 4: Supplemental (Months 12–18)

● EGF carcinogenicity risk assessment (literature-based) — 8 weeks
● Syn-Ake PK study (Franz cell + rat in vivo) — 8 weeks
● Final biocompatibility evaluation report — 4 weeks
Total estimated timeline: 18 months from study initiation to final biocompatibility evaluation report. Critical path: 52-week implantation study (PLLA microspheres). V1 510(k) submission may proceed with interim 26-week implantation data if Phase 1–2 results are favorable.
