Process validation protocol per FDA 2011 Guidance for Industry: Process Validation: General Principles and Practices. Applicable to the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch manufacturing process.

1.0 Purpose

To define the process validation approach for the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch manufacturing process using the FDA 2011 three-stage lifecycle model. This protocol ensures that the manufacturing process consistently produces product meeting its predetermined specifications and quality attributes.

2.0 Scope

This protocol covers validation of all seven manufacturing stages defined in BSK-DMR-001, from solution preparation through packaging. It applies to all BASILISK patch variants manufactured at the designated production facility.

6.0 Stage 2 — Process Qualification

6.1 Qualification Approach

Each unit operation and its associated equipment shall undergo Installation Qualification (IQ), Operational Qualification (OQ), and Performance Qualification (PQ) before commercial manufacturing begins.

6.3 Process Performance Qualification (PPQ)

PPQ shall demonstrate that the commercial manufacturing process performs as expected under routine conditions. The following requirements apply:

RequirementDetail
Number of consecutive batchesMinimum 3 consecutive conforming batches
Batch sizeCommercial scale (or justified smaller scale with scale-up rationale)
Sampling planMinimum n = 10 patches per batch per test (see Section 6.4)
PersonnelProduction operators trained per SOPs; validation team oversees
EquipmentAll equipment qualified (IQ/OQ complete) prior to PPQ
Raw materialsAll materials tested and released per specifications
DocumentationExecuted batch records with all IPC and test data

7.0 Stage 3 — Continued Process Verification

To provide ongoing assurance that the validated process remains in a state of control during routine commercial manufacturing. This stage continues throughout the product lifecycle.

7.3 Revalidation Triggers

The process shall be revalidated (partial or full PPQ) when any of the following occur:

TriggerRevalidation Scope
Change in raw material supplier (critical)PPQ for affected stages
Change in equipment (same type)IQ/OQ + abbreviated PPQ
Change in equipment (different type)Full IQ/OQ/PQ + PPQ (3 batches)
Change in manufacturing siteFull validation (all stages)
Change in batch size (> 10x)PPQ at new scale
Process parameter change outside validated rangeOQ + PPQ for affected stage
Formulation changeFull validation
Sustained adverse trend in CPV dataInvestigation first; PPQ if root cause is process-related
