9. DESIGN CHANGES — 21 CFR 820.30(i)

Purpose: All changes to approved design inputs, outputs, or processes shall be documented, reviewed, and approved through the change control procedure before implementation.

9.1 Change Control Procedure

Step 1: Change Request (CR) initiated by any team member via ECO form
Step 2: Impact assessment — effect on design inputs, outputs, risk file, regulatory status, validated processes
Step 3: Review by affected functions (R&D, QA, Regulatory, Manufacturing as applicable)
Step 4: Classification:
 MAJOR — Affects safety, efficacy, or regulatory classification; requires design review + re-verification/validation
 MODERATE — Affects performance specifications; requires re-verification of affected outputs
 MINOR — Editorial, cosmetic, or process optimization within validated ranges; QA review sufficient
Step 5: Approval (level determined by classification)
Step 6: Implementation and verification of change effectiveness
Step 7: DHF update — all affected documents revised; traceability matrix updated
