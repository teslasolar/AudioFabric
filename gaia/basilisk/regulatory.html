This document defines the dual-track regulatory strategy for BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch: V1 cosmetic/device (no 5-ALA) and V2 combination drug-device (with 5-ALA).

1.0 Purpose

To establish the regulatory pathway strategy for market authorization of the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch in the United States, European Union, and key Asia-Pacific markets. This document defines two parallel development tracks reflecting the distinct regulatory classifications of V1 (cosmetic device) and V2 (combination drug-device product).

4.0 V1 Regulatory Pathway — Cosmetic Device (No 5-ALA)

4.1 Product Classification Analysis

V1 delivers cosmetic ingredients via dissolving microneedle array. Regulatory classification depends on intended use claims and mechanism of action:

Classification OptionBasisImplications
Cosmetic (FD&C Act §201(i))Product intended to beautify/alter appearance; no drug claims. Microneedle is delivery vehicle only.No premarket clearance. Subject to MoCRA. Facility registration, adverse event reporting, product listing, GMP.
Medical Device — 510(k)If FDA determines microneedle penetration makes this a device. Predicate: dermal microneedling devices (Class II, product code QMT).510(k) clearance required. ~6-12 months. Substantial equivalence to predicate.
Medical Device — De NovoIf no suitable predicate exists for dissolving drug-loaded microneedle classified as device.De Novo classification request. ~12-18 months. Must demonstrate low-to-moderate risk.

4.1.1 Recommended V1 Strategy

Primary: File as cosmetic under MoCRA. Limit all claims to structure/function language ("reduces the appearance of fine lines"). Avoid any therapeutic or drug-type claims.

Contingency: If FDA issues enforcement action or guidance classifying dissolving microneedle cosmetics as devices, pivot to 510(k) using dermal microneedling predicates (e.g., SkinPen, product code QMT, K200357).

5.0 V2 Regulatory Pathway — Combination Drug-Device (With 5-ALA)

5.1 Product Jurisdiction

V2 contains 5-aminolevulinic acid, an FDA-approved drug substance (Levulan Kerastick, NDA 20-965). The product's primary mode of action (PMOA) is pharmacological (photodynamic therapy via protoporphyrin IX generation). Per 21 CFR Part 3, CDER has primary jurisdiction; CDRH provides device review input.

A Request for Designation (RFD) should be submitted to the Office of Combination Products (OCP) if jurisdictional ambiguity exists.

5.2 505(b)(2) NDA Strategy

The 505(b)(2) pathway permits reliance on FDA's prior findings of safety and efficacy for the listed drug (Levulan, NDA 20-965) while submitting new data for the novel formulation and delivery system.

6.0 European Union Strategy

6.1 V1 — EU Classification

OptionRegulationClassificationRequirements
Cosmetic ProductEC 1223/2009CosmeticCPNP notification, CPSR (Cosmetic Product Safety Report), responsible person designation, GMP (ISO 22716)
Medical DeviceEU MDR 2017/745Class IIa (Rule 5 — invasive, transient use)CE marking via Notified Body, clinical evaluation (MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev 4), QMS (ISO 13485), UDI-DI registration in EUDAMED

Recommended: Launch V1 as cosmetic in EU. Microneedle penetration depth (<500 μm) and dissolving nature support cosmetic classification if claims remain cosmetic. Monitor EU regulatory guidance on microneedle classification.

6.2 V2 — EU Classification

V2 with 5-ALA is a medicinal product under Directive 2001/83/EC. Requires Marketing Authorization via national procedure, Mutual Recognition Procedure (MRP), or Decentralised Procedure (DCP). Clinical trial authorization per Regulation (EU) No 536/2014.

Alternative EU strategy: Use 5-ALA phosphate salt instead of HCl. 5-ALA phosphate is not an approved drug substance in EU and may be classifiable as a cosmetic active at low concentrations. Requires legal opinion and pre-submission consultation with competent authority.
