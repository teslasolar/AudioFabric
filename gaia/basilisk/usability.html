This document defines the usability engineering process for the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch per IEC 62366-1:2015 and FDA Human Factors guidance.

1.0 Purpose

To establish the human factors engineering (HFE) / usability engineering process for the BASILISK dissolving microneedle patch in compliance with IEC 62366-1:2015 (Application of usability engineering to medical devices) and FDA guidance "Applying Human Factors and Usability Engineering to Medical Devices" (2016). This document covers use specification, hazard-related use scenarios, risk analysis, and evaluation planning.

2.0 Scope

This HFE file applies to the BASILISK patch in all commercial configurations (V1 cosmetic, V2 drug-device) including:

  • The microneedle patch (primary product)
  • Primary packaging (foil pouch with tear notch)
  • Secondary packaging (outer carton)
  • Instructions for Use (IFU) insert
  • Quick-start guide (if included)

4.3 Use Scenarios

Scenario 1: First-Time Use (Naive User)

Context: 42-year-old female, purchased online, first microneedle product. Reading IFU for the first time. Home bathroom, evening.

Critical factors: Must understand microneedle concept without alarm. Must correctly identify needle side vs. backing. Must apply adequate pressure. Must understand 15-minute wait time.

Success criteria: Completes application without critical use errors. Reports experience as manageable and would use again.

Scenario 2: Repeat Use (Experienced User)

Context: Same user, 4th application. Familiar with process. May skip IFU. Home bathroom, morning before work.

Critical factors: Time pressure may cause rushed application. May attempt to multitask during 15-minute wait. Complacency with pressure application.

Success criteria: Maintains correct technique. Does not develop unsafe shortcuts.

Scenario 3: Travel Use

Context: 55-year-old male, hotel bathroom, limited counter space. Brought patches in carry-on. No IFU available (discarded outer carton).

Critical factors: Must identify correct application without IFU (pouch-printed quick-start). Storage conditions may have varied during travel.

Success criteria: Pouch label provides sufficient instruction for correct application.

Scenario 4: Dexterity-Limited User

Context: 63-year-old female with mild rheumatoid arthritis. Reduced grip strength and fine motor control.

Critical factors: Pouch opening (tear notch). Patch removal from liner (peeling). Positioning on face (periorbital area). Maintaining pressure for 30 seconds.

Success criteria: Completes application without assistance. Pouch opens without scissors or tools.

8.3 Formative Iteration Criteria

After each formative round, design modifications are implemented if:

  • Any participant commits a critical use error (wrong orientation, contraindicated site)
  • >25% of participants struggle with the same non-critical task
  • Participants express confusion or uncertainty about a critical task (even if completed correctly)
  • Close calls observed (participant nearly commits error, self-corrects)

Formative studies do not have pass/fail criteria. Their purpose is to identify use difficulties and guide design improvements before summative validation.
